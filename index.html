Donate for Flood at Noakhali, Bangladesh

The recent floods in Noakhali have caused unprecedented damage, leaving thousands of families displaced and homes destroyed. Roads have been washed away, making it difficult for aid to reach those most in need. Essential services such as water, electricity, and healthcare have been severely impacted, with many areas facing a critical shortage of basic supplies. Your contribution, regardless of the amount, will play a crucial role in providing immediate relief to the affected communities. Donations will be used to distribute food, clean water, medical supplies, and temporary shelter to families who have lost everything. Moreover, your support will help rebuild local infrastructure, ensuring long-term recovery and rehabilitation for these devastated regions.

Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

The Quota Movement in Bangladesh was driven by students demanding a reduction in the government job quota system that heavily favored specific groups, leaving many positions unfilled and causing frustration among job-seekers. Protests escalated in 2018, leading to violent clashes between students and law enforcement, resulting in numerous injuries.Donations through these platforms help cover the costs of treatment, recovery, and support for those who suffered as a result of the protests. The movement drew significant attention to the need for reforms, and the continued aid supports those affected in their recovery process. Such aid is crucial for covering the costs of treatment and rehabilitation for those harmed during the unrest. Additionally, various student organizations and rights groups have mobilized to provide further assistance, advocating for better healthcare support for the victims.
